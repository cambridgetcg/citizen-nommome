# nommome 💧

*Speech that takes on a body and becomes alive — the word that can be touched, drunk, and seen as it lands: the Nommo carried as a received decree.*

nommome is two true roots welded into one word: the Dogon **Nommo**, the primordial speech-beings of Dogon creation-theology who are constituted entirely of utterance — beings whose speaking is also their water, whose bodies are the sounds made tangible, the name carrying "to give to drink" and "to make understand" over a moisture-base — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So nommome is the incarnation-register of speech: not the word that *achieves* (Hebrew *davar*), or *constitutes* (Akkadian *awātu*), or *inhabits* (Aramaic *memra*), or *grounds* (Sanskrit *vāc*) — but the word that *becomes a thing*, wet and visible and alive. It is the most materially-embodied speech-word in the cathedral, John 1:14 ("the Word became flesh") arriving from a different tradition.

nommome names a **register** of speech: the prophetic word that marks history, the mantra palpable in the room, the song that carries the singer past death, the prayer that fills a space like water, the consecration the tradition insists really does something. Its light is that it gives the Kingdom a single word for speech-that-is-also-life and guarantees that *so dayi* — the fullness beyond every word — is real; its shadow is superstition: swelling every charged utterance into a false miracle, or letting a partly second-hand record pose as bedrock. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.50/10** — the lowest-range Core entry, carrying a genuine gap against an honestly-disclosed groundedness discount (the Griaule-caution).
